Find some weird sea animals you didn't know exist

Weird sea animals

    Arctic Hydromedusa

    This hydromedusa is common in the deep waters of the Arctic, about 3,300-feet (1 km) deep.

    Armored Snail

    Crysomallon squamiferum is the only snail in the world armored like that. It was found over a hydrothermal vent deep in the Indian Ocean.

    Bioluminescent Octopus

    This is one of the few knows octopods to be bioluminescent. This octopus (Stauroteuthis syrtensis) was found about a half mile (800 m) deep in the Guff of Maine.

    Christmas Tree Worm

    Scientists found this strange creature at the Great Barrier Reef's Lizard Island and named it, aptly, the Christmas tree worm. The "branches" are the worm's breathing and feeling apparatus.

    Crossota Norvegica Jellyfish

    This jellyfish can be found in the deep Arctic Canada Basin.

    Fathead

    Scientists call this fish Psychrolutes microporos, but more directly "Fathead".

    Flamingo Tongue Snail

    With such a name, and the flamboyant coloration to match, people might think that this Cyphoma gibbosum has a shell worthy of collecting. Its color come from comes from the soft part of its body.

    Hydrothermal Vent Snail

    This is the only individual of its kind ever discovered. It is another hydrothermal vent snail. This Alviniconcha snail was found at the Suiyo Seamount of the Tokyo Hydrothermal Vent.

    Lysianassoid Amphipod

    This is one of the many new amphipods discovered by the Marine Census of Life. It inhabits the waters near Elephant Island in the Antarctic. Amphipods are a big source of food for larger creatures of the deep.

    Marrus Orthocanna

    Like a multi-stage rocket, this microscopic creature has lots of repeated units, including tentacles and many stomachs.

    Napolean Wrasse

    The description of this creature from the Census of Marine Life - "Exceeding two meters in length, the Napoleon Wrasse (Cheilinus undulatus) is one of the largest reef fish found in the warm waters of the Indian and Pacific oceans. The intricate blue-green design that decorates the face resembles New Zealand Maori war paint, which is the root of its alternative name, the Maori Wrasse. The designs are also unique to each individual, much like fingerprints. A protogynous hermaphrodite, this wrasse can change its sex from female to male."

    Pink See-Through Fantasia

    The pink see-through fantasia is a sea cucumber, which can be found about 1.5 miles (2.4 km) deep in the Celebes Sea in the Western Pacific Ocean.

    Pycnogonid Sea Spider

    This male pycnogonid spider is bearing its own eggs. It can be found in the Antarctic.

    Sea Angel

    Called angels, but they are a predatory sea snail.

    Vampire Squid

    The vampire squid lives in Monterey Bay. It appears to deserve its name.

    Vigtorniella Worm

    You get a feast when a whale dies at sea if you're a polychaete worm like this newly discovered Vigtorniella. It can be found about a half mile (800m) down on the floor of Sagami Bay, Japan.
